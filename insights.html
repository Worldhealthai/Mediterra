How to Shuck Mussels

Step 1: Rinse mussels under cold water, scrubbing shells to remove debris.

Step 2: Remove the "beard" (byssal threads) by pulling it toward the hinge.

Step 3: Discard any mussels with broken shells or that don't close when tapped.

Step 4: For raw preparation, use a shucking knife to gently pry open at the hinge.

Pro tip: Most recipes call for cooking mussels in their shells ‚Äî they'll open naturally when steamed!

Cooking & Preparation

Steaming: 5-7 minutes in white wine, garlic, and herbs until shells open.

Grilling: 3-5 minutes on high heat with butter and lemon.

Pasta: Add to sauces in the last 5 minutes of cooking.

Soups & Stews: Add near the end to prevent overcooking.

Golden rule: Discard any mussels that don't open after cooking ‚Äî they're not safe to eat.
